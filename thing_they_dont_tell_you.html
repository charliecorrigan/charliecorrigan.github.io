The Thing They Don't Tell You About Programming


24 April 2017


My first six weeks at Turing have been an intensive crash course in programming fundamentals. I've formed a solid foundation in general programming skills and started to become comfortable in Ruby specifically. But, I'm also learning what it's like to be able to program a machine. It's absolutely wonderous.


Because I don't have a very traditional background for a software engineer, I can't help but feel like a spy that's been sent into foreign territory with an obligation to report back to my fellow liberal arts and English majors what programming is really like.


"'Harry - yer a wizard.'
'I'm a what?' gasped Harry."
- JK Rowling, Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone


Here's the big secret that no one told me as I was reading fantasy novels and building worlds with paint as liberal arts major: Programming is our world's wizardry. You learn to speak a new language that can communicate with a non-human source of power, and you draw on that source of power to make interesting and powerful things happen in the world around you.


I am learning to be a mother fucking wizard. And it is everything I dreamed it might be.
